Major Tribes of Nigeria

Hausa-Fulani

Hausa–Fulani are people of mixed Hausa and Fulani origin. They are primarily found in the Northern region of Nigeria, most of whom speak a variant of Hausa or Fula or both as their first language. While some Fulani claim Semitic origins, Hausas are indigenous to West Africa.

Yoruba

Their language is tonal and spoken widely across Nigeria. Yoruba communities traditionally live in agricultural compounds, and many practice their native religion featuring a vast pantheon of deities. The Yoruba are also famed for their music, defined by one of the most complex drumming cultures in the world.

Igbo

Igbo make up around 18 per cent of Nigeria's population. Their traditional homeland straddles the Niger River in the south-east and is one of the most densely populated areas of the African continent. Igbo are predominantly Christian. Traditionally, Igbo were subsistence farmers of yams, taro and cassava.

Nigerian Folk Tales

The Lying Shepherd

Once upon a time there was a shepherd with his sheep. As they were on the grassland and the sheep were grazing the shepherd started to feel bored. Eventually he decided to trick some people from the near village by shouting loudly: "Help, help, am being attacked by wolves, somebody help!". After some short time, some of the village inhabitans were running to that grassland. Suddenly the shepherd bursted into laughter and told them that it was only a joke. Of course the inhabitants were very angry but they just left the place. The next day it was just the same thing again: the shepherd was shepherding the sheep and after some time starting to feel bored and he shouted for help again. Traditional Nigerian Foods

Jollof Rice

Jollof Rice is one of Nigeria's most beloved and iconic traditional dishes. Jollof rice is a vibrant, one-pot dish made from rice, tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices. It has a rich red-orange color due to the tomato sauce, and it's often served as the centerpiece at celebrations, parties, and family gatherings. In Nigeria, Jollof rice is a national favorite, and it has become part of the country's culinary identity.

Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup

Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup is one of Nigeria's most cherished and traditional meals, particularly popular among the Yoruba, Igbo, and other ethnic groups in the southwestern and southeastern regions of the country. It is a dish that represents Nigerian hospitality and is often enjoyed during special occasions, family gatherings, and festive events.

Ogbono Soup and Akpu

Ogbono Soup and Akpu is another deeply traditional and cherished dish in Nigeria, particularly popular among the Igbo people in the southeastern part of the country. This meal combines the rich, hearty flavors of Ogbono soup, which is made from wild African mango seeds, with the starchy and smooth Akpu (a type of cassava fufu), creating a satisfying and nutritious dish that is enjoyed by many.

Koko and Kosai

Koko and Kosai is a traditional Nigerian breakfast combination that is particularly popular in the northern regions of Nigeria, where it is a common street food. This pair is a delicious, filling, and affordable meal, especially loved by the Hausa and Fulani people.

Top Tourist Attractions in Nigeria

Nike Art Attraction

Nike Art Gallery is one of the most prominent and fascinating tourist attractions in Nigeria, located in Lagos. It is renowned as one of the largest art galleries in West Africa, showcasing a rich and diverse collection of traditional and contemporary African art. The gallery is a hub for art lovers, cultural enthusiasts, and tourists looking to experience the vibrancy of Nigerian culture through its artistic expressions.

Olumo Rock

Olumo Rock is one of Nigeria's most famous and historically significant tourist attractions, located in Abeokuta, the capital of Ogun State. It is a massive granite rock formation that stands approximately 137 meters above sea level and holds immense cultural and historical importance, especially for the Egba people of southwestern Nigeria. Olumo Rock is not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of strength, unity, and protection for the Egba people.

Erin Ijesha

Erin Ijesha Waterfall, also known as Olumirin Waterfalls, is one of Nigeria’s most stunning and popular natural tourist attractions. Located in Erin Ijesha, a town in Osun State, the waterfall is a breathtaking cascade of water flowing from the top of a high cliff, creating multiple levels of falls and natural pools. It is not only a place of scenic beauty but also holds deep cultural and historical significance for the Yoruba people, particularly the Ijesha community.

Nigerian Festivals

Argungu Fishing Festival

The Argungu Fishing Festival is one of Nigeria’s most celebrated and unique cultural events, held annually in Argungu, a town in Kebbi State, northern Nigeria. The festival is a vibrant celebration of culture, tradition, and community, centered around a competitive fishing event that attracts thousands of spectators and participants from across Nigeria and beyond. It is one of the oldest and most popular festivals in the country, recognized for its rich cultural significance, colorful displays, and festive atmosphere.

Ojude Oba

Ojude Oba is a vibrant cultural festival celebrated annually by the Ijebu people of Ogun State in southwestern Nigeria. The festival, which takes place on the third day after the Islamic festival of Eid-el-Kabir (also known as the Sallah festival), is a colorful and significant event that showcases the rich cultural heritage of the Ijebu community.

Eyo Festival

The Eyo Festival, also known as the Adamu Orisha Play, is one of Lagos' most iconic and culturally significant festivals. It is celebrated on Lagos Island and is rooted in the customs of the Yoruba people. The festival showcases the rich traditions of the indigenous Lagosians (Isale Eko) and is often considered a forerunner to modern-day carnivals like the Rio Carnival in Brazil.

Osun Festival

The Osun-Osogbo Festival, also known as the Osun Festival, is one of Nigeria's most important traditional and cultural events. It is an annual festival held in Osogbo, the capital city of Osun State, to honor Osun, the Yoruba goddess of fertility, love, and water. The festival is celebrated in the sacred Osun Sacred Grove, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and is a key event that attracts worshippers, tourists, and dignitaries from Nigeria and around the world.
